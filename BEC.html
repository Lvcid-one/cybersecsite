Business Email Compromise (BEC)

(Also known as Email Account Compromise (EAC)) A type of phishing attack where a threat actor impersonates a known source to obtain financial advantage. It exploits the fact so many of use rely on email to conduct business - both personal and professional.

How it works:

Threat actors either spoof or hack into a CEO's email account, then email employees instructions to make a purchase or send money via wire transfer. The threat actor may even ask an employee to purchase gift cards, then request photos of the serial numbers.
Some examples include:

  • A vendor a company regularly deals with sends an invoice with an updated mailing address.
  • A company CEO asks an assistant to purchase dozens of gift cards to send out as employee rewards. She asks for the serial numbers so she can email them out right away.
  • A homebuyer receives a message for a title company with instructions on how to wire his down payment.

These are only some examples on how a BEC can be carried out that affect both organizations and individuals.
